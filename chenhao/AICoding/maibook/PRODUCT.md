# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Academic researchers, graduate students, and knowledge workers who regularly read, annotate, and analyze scientific papers. They work in focused reading sessions, often processing multiple papers per session. Primary context: desktop, alone, in deep concentration.

## Product Purpose

Maibook is a local-first academic paper reader that augments the reading experience with AI. It closes the loop from paper discovery to reading comprehension to group-meeting preparation. Core jobs: read PDFs, annotate key passages, translate text, get AI explanations for complex content, and manage a personal paper library.

## Brand Personality

Professional, efficient, reliable. The interface should feel like a well-made research tool: quiet, restrained, and focused on the content. No decorative flair; every element serves the reading and analysis workflow. Trustworthy and academically credible, not trendy or playful.

## Anti-references

- No marketing-landing-page patterns: no hero sections, no gradient cards, no animated illustrations
- No dark-mode-first "cool tool" aesthetic; default is light, paper-like
- No gamification, streaks, or engagement tricks
- No overly rounded, soft, or consumer-app visuals

## Design Principles

1. **Content-first**: The paper is always the priority; UI recedes
2. **One task at a time**: Each surface has a clear primary action
3. **Efficiency through familiarity**: Standard patterns, predictable layouts, low cognitive load
4. **Quiet confidence**: Restrained color, minimal motion, professional typography
5. **Offline-capable**: Everything works without a network; AI is additive, not essential

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA for all core interactions
- Keyboard navigable for power users
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations
- High-contrast text on reading surfaces
- Bilingual support (Chinese / English) for international academic audience
